🍽️ Understanding the Glycemic Index

The Glycemic Index (GI) measures how quickly foods raise blood sugar levels. Choosing low-GI foods can help manage diabetes by providing steady energy without blood sugar spikes.

✅ Low GI Foods (0-55)

Best choices for blood sugar control

💡 Tips for Managing Glycemic Impact

🥗 Combine Foods

Pair high-GI foods with protein, fat, or fiber to slow glucose absorption.

🍳 Consider Cooking Methods

Al dente pasta has a lower GI than fully cooked. Raw foods often have lower GI than cooked.

🍌 Watch Ripeness

Riper fruits have higher GI. Choose slightly underripe bananas for lower impact.

📏 Portion Control

Even low-GI foods can raise blood sugar if eaten in large quantities. Watch your portions.

⚠️ These GI values are approximate and can vary based on preparation and individual factors. Always consult with your healthcare provider or registered dietitian for personalized nutrition advice.
