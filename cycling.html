🚴 Cycling with Diabetes

Cycling is an excellent low-impact exercise for people with diabetes. It helps improve cardiovascular health, aids in weight management, and can improve insulin sensitivity. Follow these tips to cycle safely while managing your blood sugar levels.

🗺️ Explore Cycling Map

⚠️ Stop Cycling Immediately If You Experience

These may be signs of low blood sugar:

• Shaking or trembling • Excessive sweating • Dizziness or lightheadedness • Confusion • Rapid heartbeat • Blurred vision • Extreme fatigue

💡 Route Planning Tips for Diabetics

🏁 After Your Ride

Check Glucose

Test immediately and again 2–4 hours later

Eat a Snack

Have protein + carbs within 30 minutes

Stay Hydrated

Continue drinking water after cycling

Log Your Ride

Record distance, time, and glucose levels

🚴 Cycling Safety Rules

Test Before • Carry Carbs • Stay Hydrated • Ride Smart
